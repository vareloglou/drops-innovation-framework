The Name
Why DROPS & WAVES?

Innovation isn't one big bang. It's disciplined, repeatable, compounding. Every innovation bet starts as a Drop — small, intentional, precise. But drops don't stay small. The ones that work become Waves that reshape entire categories. The name captures both dimensions: the discipline of starting small, and the ambition of scaling big.

💧

It's distinctly beverage

The language is native to the business — liquid, flow, volume, impact. No consultant jargon, no borrowed tech metaphors. It belongs here.

⚙️

It's an operating language

Not just a name on a slide. Teams can actually use it: "How many Drops in pipeline?" "This Drop is ready to scale into a Wave." It works in a meeting, not just a boardroom.

📈

It captures the full journey

DROPS is the discipline of starting small. WAVES is the ambition of scaling to category impact. Together they describe the complete innovation lifecycle — from idea to transformation.

The Culture
Innovation Beliefs

The operating model defines how innovation runs. These beliefs define the culture that fuels it. Without these, DROPS & WAVES is a process. With them, it's a way of working.

01

Start with real human needs

Every Drop begins with a genuine consumer problem or desire — not a technology looking for a use case, not a trend looking for a product. Human first, always.

02

Simplify to stand out

In a world of overwhelming choice, the innovations that win are the ones that make life simpler, not more complex. Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication.

03

Be brave enough to break patterns

The most valuable innovations come from challenging assumptions that everyone else accepts as truth. Courage to break category conventions is a competitive advantage.

04

Iterate before you scale

Nothing is born big. Test small, learn fast, improve continuously. The discipline to iterate is what separates innovation from gambling. This is the core of DROPS.

05

Play to imagine what's next

Innovation requires space for exploration without immediate commercial pressure. Playfulness isn't a luxury — it's the engine that generates the unexpected ideas that become Waves.

06

Don't let the future happen — Shape it

The defining belief. Innovation isn't about reacting to change. It's about creating the change. DROPS & WAVES is the mechanism for shaping the future of beverages, not waiting for it.

Refresh What's Next. — These six beliefs are the cultural engine behind DROPS & WAVES. The operating model provides the structure. The beliefs provide the soul.
What the Best Companies Do
Every winning innovator has a named
methodology with a clear operating language

We researched P&G, Unilever, AB InBev, Heineken, Asahi, and Amazon. Here's what to learn from each.

P&G
Connect + Develop

35%+ of innovations from external partners. Five-vector Superiority Model as decision framework across 65 brands. AI powers 65% of product development. Tripled success rate to 50–60%.

→ Learn: The Superiority Model as a decision framework. Transaction Learning Experiments — small bets before big scaling.
Unilever
Fewer, Bigger, Better

Cut innovation projects by 40%, doubled average project size. 12 innovation platforms each targeting €100M+ revenue. Foundry as venture-client platform (Pitch-Pilot-Partner).

→ Learn: The platform model — 12 platforms at €100M each vs. 100 scattered projects. The Pitch-Pilot-Partner cadence.
AB InBev
ZX Ventures

Separate venture engine with own HR, P&L, rules. 12-week accelerator cycles. $1B+ in venture sales. BEES B2B platform captures 72% of company revenue.

→ Learn: The 12-week cycle as speed benchmark. Innovation includes business model innovation, not just product.
Heineken
EverGreen 2030

€1B Digital Backbone investment. Three-pillar strategy: Accelerate Growth, Step Up Productivity, Future-Proof. Heineken 0.0 grew 53% organically 2020–2024.

→ Learn: Three pillars (Growth / Productivity / Future-Proofing) as strategic organizer. The "big bet" approach with 0.0.
Amazon
Working Backwards

Start with the consumer press release, then build the product. Two-pizza autonomous teams. "Type 1 vs Type 2" decision framework — most innovation is reversible, so move fast.

→ Learn: Write the consumer announcement before the brief. Forces clarity on "why does anyone care?"
Asahi
Backcasting from the Future

Envision ideal society 10 years forward, work backwards to select R&D themes. Innovation crosses category boundaries — from beer to feminine care supplements.

→ Learn: The backcasting methodology. And the courage to innovate beyond category boundaries.
