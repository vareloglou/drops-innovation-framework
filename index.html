The Name
Why DROPS?

Innovation at Coca-Cola isn't one big bang. It's disciplined, repeatable, compounding. Every innovation bet is a Drop — small, intentional, precise. But drops don't stay small.

💧

It's distinctly beverage

Without being forced. The language is native to the business — liquid, flow, volume, impact. No consultant jargon, no borrowed tech metaphors.

⚙️

It's an operating language

Not just a name on a slide. Teams can actually use it: "How many Drops in pipeline?" "This Drop is ready to scale." "Let's kill this Drop and reallocate." It works in a meeting, not just a boardroom.

📈

It signals discipline

Drops are precise and intentional — not random splashes. It frames innovation as portfolio management, not innovation theater. Exactly what investors want to hear.

The Language
Nothing is born big.
Everything starts as a Drop.

DROPS is a progression, not a category system. Every innovation — no matter how ambitious — enters the system as a Drop. It has to earn its way forward. The difference between innovations isn't what kind of bet they are. It's how far they travel.

The Operating Language
The life of a Drop

Every innovation enters as a Drop and has to earn its way forward. Most Drops get killed — that's the point. The ones that survive create Ripples. The ones that compound become Streams. And very rarely, a Stream becomes a Wave.

1

Source the Drop

Where does the idea come from? Hubs, R&D, partners, consumer insights, market signals.

→
2

Shape the Drop

Is it viable? Prototype, stress-test the concept, define success criteria.

→
3

Test the Drop

Does it work? Small-scale market experiment. Real consumers, real data, real signal.

→
4

Watch for Ripples

Is it spreading? Look for organic pull — cross-market interest, cross-brand application, partner demand.

→
5

Build the Stream

It's compounding. Dedicate resources, create a pipeline of Drops feeding it, scale deliberately.

→
✕

Kill the Drop

At any stage: does this still earn its place? Kill fast, reallocate, learn. No zombie projects.

Proposed Evolution
What we'd strengthen in the current draft

Add "how it runs" alongside "what it is"

The four pillars describe what innovation is. Currently missing: how the machine operates — stages, gates, cycle time, team model. DROPS adds the engine layer.

Elevate Process & Metrics from enabler to engine

Currently: Process and Metrics sit at the bottom as "enablers." Proposed: They become the central operating system. At P&G, AB InBev, Unilever — the process IS the innovation.

Introduce a progression model

Currently: no distinction between early-stage experiments and proven platforms. Proposed: Every innovation starts as a Drop and earns its way to Ripple → Stream → Wave. Different management, different metrics at each stage. Nothing is born big.

Build in a kill mechanism

Risk: New functions try to prove value by running too many things. DROPS installs: explicit gates at every stage. Kill fast, reallocate, no zombie projects. Unilever cut 40% of projects and doubled impact.

Add a time dimension

Currently: no distinction between near-term and long-term innovation. Proposed: "Strengthen the Core" (protect and grow existing) vs. "Create the New" (new spaces). Inspired by P&G's model.

Embed AI across all pillars

Currently: AI lives implicitly inside "Edge" (Tech). Proposed: AI as infrastructure across all four pillars — consumer insights (Human), product dev (Versatile), R&D methodology (Edge), partner discovery (Networked).

What the Best Companies Do
Every winning innovator has a named
methodology with a clear operating language

We researched P&G, Unilever, AB InBev, Heineken, Asahi, Amazon, Apple, Tesla, Google, and Samsung. Here's what to steal.

P&G
Connect + Develop

35%+ of innovations from external partners. Five-vector "Superiority Model" as decision framework across 65 brands. AI powers 65% of product development. Tripled success rate to 50-60%.

→ Steal: The Superiority Model as a decision framework. Transaction Learning Experiments — small bets before big scaling.
Unilever
Fewer, Bigger, Better

Cut innovation projects by 40%, doubled average project size. 12 innovation platforms each targeting €100M+ revenue. Foundry as venture-client platform (Pitch-Pilot-Partner).

→ Steal: The platform model — 12 platforms at €100M each vs. 100 scattered projects. The Pitch-Pilot-Partner cadence.
AB InBev
ZX Ventures

Separate venture engine with own HR, P&L, rules. 12-week accelerator cycles. $1B+ in venture sales. BEES B2B platform captures 72% of company revenue.

→ Steal: The 12-week cycle as speed benchmark. Innovation includes business model innovation, not just product.
Heineken
EverGreen 2030

€1B Digital Backbone investment. Three-pillar strategy: Accelerate Growth, Step Up Productivity, Future-Proof. Heineken 0.0 grew 53% organically 2020-2024.

→ Steal: Three pillars (Growth / Productivity / Future-Proofing) as strategic organizer. The "big bet" approach with 0.0.
Amazon
Working Backwards

Start with the consumer press release, then build the product. Two-pizza autonomous teams. "Type 1 vs Type 2" decision framework — most innovation is reversible, so move fast.

→ Steal: Write the consumer announcement before the brief. Forces clarity on "why does anyone care?"
Asahi
Backcasting from the Future

Envision ideal society 10 years forward, work backwards to select R&D themes. Innovation crosses category boundaries — from beer to feminine care supplements.

→ Steal: The backcasting methodology. And the courage to innovate beyond category boundaries.
